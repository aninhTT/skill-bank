# Offsite & Workshop Templates
Source: DGLT Offsite (April 6), EFLT AI Workshop, SLT AI Workshop

---

## How to Choose the Right Template

| Audience + Goals | Template |
|-----------------|---------|
| Leadership team (Director+); focused on individual productivity, advanced use cases, and how to lead AI change in their org | **DGLT Full-Day** |
| Finance/Ops-type leadership team; focused on hands-on building (3–4 skills + mini assistant) | **EFLT 3.5-Hour** |
| Senior leadership (VP+/SLT); focused on building AI skills, expanding sense of possibility, and making public commitments | **SLT Full-Day** |

---

## Template 1: DGLT Full-Day AI Workshop

**Audience:** Director-level leadership team
**Length:** Full day (~7.5 hrs including lunch)
**Goals:**
- Get unstuck on real use cases each leader has been trying
- Evaluate AI use case portfolio as a group
- Connect individual productivity to H2 planning and roadmapping implications
- Each leader leaves with a specific commitment

### Pre-Work
- Each participant brings **one real use case** they've tried to automate or hit a wall on
- Compile a **group-wide use case inventory** in advance (Amie or facilitator aggregates)
- Participants should arrive with at least basic tool familiarity (TackStart or equivalent)

---

### Morning — Track A: Advanced Individual Productivity

**9:00–9:30 — Opening + Framing**
- Welcome; set the tone for the day
- Group agreements: "It's okay to be stuck." "We're here to learn, not to perform."
- Icebreaker: "In one sentence — what's your current relationship with AI at work?"
- Brief orientation to the day's structure

**9:30–11:00 — Getting Unstuck (Small Group Work)**
- Break into groups of 3–4
- Each person shares: What use case am I trying? Where am I stuck?
- Groups help each other iterate — try things live, suggest different prompts or tools, trouble-shoot
- Optional: open Q&A channel running in parallel (Slack thread or async option for questions that come up)
- Facilitator circulates; notes common patterns

**11:00–11:15 — Break**

**11:15–11:30 — Additional Personal Productivity Build Time**
- Unstructured; continue individual work from the morning session
- Facilitator available for 1:1 help

**11:30–12:00 — Share-Out**
- Each group shares: What did we get unstuck on? What surprised us?
- Open floor Q&A
- Note common themes for afternoon discussion

---

### Lunch: 12:00–1:00

---

### Early Afternoon — Track B: Direction, Ownership & Next Steps

**1:00–2:00 — From Personal Productivity to Group Impact**
- Walk through the full group use case inventory compiled in pre-work
- Introduce a simple use case evaluation framework: What makes a strong use case? (Consider: impact potential, feasibility, data access, team readiness)
- Group exercise: run each use case through the framework
  - What's worth prioritizing for H2?
  - What's technically or organizationally out of reach right now?
  - What's a quick win vs. a larger investment?
- Pressure-test direction as a group

**2:00–2:15 — Afternoon Break**

---

### Late Afternoon — Leadership & Change Management

**2:15–4:30 — H2 Planning and Roadmapping**
Discussion questions to work through:
- What does this AI plan mean for H2? What needs to change about what we're prioritizing?
- Are there OKR implications — things we should add, remove, or reframe?
- How do we carve explicit space for Track A (personal fluency + experimentation) alongside the roadmap?
- How does our roadmapping process itself change with AI?
- What's each leader's specific role in helping their team through H2 implications?

**4:30–4:45 — Commitments & Close**
- Each leader names one specific commitment for the next 30 days
- Confirm Track B owners + next steps
- Who's doing what, by when, accountable to whom?

## Template 2: EFLT 3.5-Hour Hands-On Build Session

**Audience:** Finance, Ops, or similar leadership team
**Length:** ~3.5 hours (typically 9:00 AM–1:00 PM, with optional extended lunch)
**Goals:**
- Each participant builds 3–4 connected skills in Claude Code / Claude Cowork
- Those skills connect into a functional "mini assistant" for their work
- Leave with something real, not just exposure

### Pre-Work
- Install the Claude desktop app
- Watch short videos: Claude app 101, Claude in Excel (15–20 min), Coding tools 101
- Complete the AI workflow mapper — identify 2–3 workflows that feel repetitive or manual
- Review the use case inspiration doc (shared before the session)

---

### Agenda

**9:00–9:20 — Welcome + Framing**
- Why we want you to feel out of your comfort zone today
- Session agreements: progress over perfection, ask questions out loud, help your neighbor
- Check in on pre-work: Did you install the app? What workflows did you map?
- Set goals for the session: what do you want to walk away with?

**9:20–10:00 — Claude in Excel: Live Demo**
- Live demonstration of spreadsheet manipulation and data analysis using Claude
- Show the full process end-to-end — not just the output
- Participants follow along on their own machines
- Pause for questions, troubleshooting, variations

**10:00–10:30 — Core Build Framing + Skill #1**
- Goal: Build 3–4 skills that connect into a single "mini assistant"
- Amie introduces the skill framework and leads discussion: What problems do you want this assistant to solve?
- **Skill 1 — Granola skill**: Connect Granola meeting summaries to an output format the team will actually use
  - Example: Auto-pull action items from last week's meetings into a formatted weekly digest
- **Skill 2 — Data analysis skill**: Upload a recurring data file and have AI produce a standard analysis or summary
- **Skills 3–4 — Team's choice**: Based on the group's own workflows and priorities

Use plan mode in Claude Code to brainstorm and scope before building.

**10:30–10:45 — Break**

**10:45–12:00 — Continue Building Skills**
- Continue Skill 2 and move to Skills 3–4 (participant-chosen)
- Facilitator circulates; help where people are stuck
- Encourage experimentation — it's okay if it doesn't work the first time
- Start connecting skills into a single flow where possible

**12:00–1:00 — Lunch (optional: continue building informally)**

**1:00–2:00 — Live Share-Out**
- Every participant demos their mini assistant: ~3 minutes each
- Structure: "Here's what it does. Here's the problem it solves. Here's what I'd do next."
- Facilitator notes common patterns across builds for team debrief

**Close + Commitments**
- How will you continue your own learning after today?
- How will you support your team's learning?
- What's your next build?

## Template 3: SLT Full-Day AI Workshop

**Audience:** Senior leadership team (VP+, SLT)
**Length:** Full day (~7.5 hrs including lunch)
**Goals:**
- Expanded sense of what's genuinely possible with AI — not just chat, but agents and workflows
- Tangible artifacts: each participant builds something real for their function
- Public learning commitments: each leader names how they'll continue and how they'll lead their team

**Definition of success:** Everyone believes they're builders. Everyone builds something for their function. Leaders make public commitments to learning and cascading.

---

### Agenda

**9:00–9:15 — Welcome + Framing**
- The AI evolution: chat → AI search → agents → multi-agent systems. Where we are now and where we're going.
- Session agreements:
  - Progress over perfection
  - Learn out loud
  - Lean into discomfort
  - Ask AI first
- Quick pulse: "Where do you feel behind? What do you wish you understood better?"

**9:15–10:30 — External Guest / Inspiration Session**
- External speaker or practitioner sharing how they're using AI at the frontier of their craft
- Focus on use cases that feel ambitious but within reach
- Q&A: What would you do with our org? What's the mistake most senior leaders make?

**10:30–10:45 — Break + Tech Check**
- Ensure all participants have Claude desktop app open and running
- Troubleshoot any access issues before the build block

**10:45–12:10 — Exercise 1: Building AI Skills (3–4 Connected Skills)**
Goal: Build skills that connect and compound — not isolated demos, but a mini system.

- **Skill 1 (Guided) — BigQuery / Data Integration**: Pull relevant data from a live source into Claude. What data do you look at most often? What question do you wish you could answer faster?
- **Skill 2 (Guided) — Granola MCP Integration**: Connect Granola meeting summaries to Claude with custom instructions. What output format would actually be useful to you after each meeting?
- **Skills 3–4 (Your Choice)**: What problems do you want visibility on? What big-picture questions could AI help you answer weekly?
  - Examples: summarize Slack activity in key channels; analyze Jira velocity against targets; generate a weekly team digest from multiple sources

**12:10–12:30 — Quick Debrief**
- What worked? What surprised you?
- What was harder than you expected?
- What question do you now have that you didn't have this morning?

**12:30–1:45 — Lunch**

**1:45–2:00 — Exercise 2 Brainstorm: Build a Mini App**
- Prompt: "If you could have any app to make your professional life better — what would it be?"
- Individual reflection; share out a few ideas
- Introduce the DVFR lens: Desirable (would you actually use it?), Viable (worth the time?), Feasible (actually buildable today?), Responsible (appropriate to build?)

**2:00–2:20 — Plan Your Build**
- Draft a lightweight PRD (product requirements document) for your mini app:
  - What problem does it solve?
  - Who's the user?
  - What are the 3–5 features it needs?
  - What data would it need to access?
  - What should the interface look like?
- Use AI to help write the PRD

**2:20–3:20 — Build Your Mini App Prototype**
- Approve your plan in Claude (use plan mode)
- Build, debug, iterate
- It doesn't need to be polished — it needs to demonstrate the idea
- Facilitator circulates; help with debugging and scoping

**3:20–3:30 — Break**

**3:30–3:40 — Future Possibilities + Limitations Discussion**
- The deployment gap: building something is different from deploying it at scale
- Org implications: what would it take to put something like this in front of your team or into a real workflow?
- What's genuinely out of reach right now, and why? (Data access, security review, maintenance, etc.)

**3:40–4:30 — Wrap-Up + Commitments**

*Reflection (10 min):*
- What was your biggest "aha" moment today?
- What are the implications for your org — how people work, what's possible, what might change?
- What barriers did you hit today that your team will hit too?

*Two Commitments Each (30 min):*
1. **How I'll continue my own learning** — specific, not vague. What tool, what cadence, what use case?
2. **How I'll support my team's learning** — what will you do differently in how you lead from this point?

*Accountability plan:*
- How will we hold each other to these? Who checks in on whom?
- When do we debrief as a group on what's changed?
