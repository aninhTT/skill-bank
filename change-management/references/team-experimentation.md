# Team Experimentation & Manager Playbook
Source: Manager AI Playbook (go/manager-ai)

---

## Manager Cadence Ideas

### Quick (Daily / Weekly)
- **5–15 min learning moments** — TackStart or TackShift lessons fit here; designed to be done in the flow of work
- **Monitor #ai-lab** — check weekly for tips and experiments from across the company; try one with your team
- **AI End-of-Day Corner** — rotating Slack share: one thing someone did with AI that day. Keep it brief, no polish required
- **Team Meeting "AI Moment"** — dedicate 5 min in your standing team meeting; one person shares: what they tried → what happened → what they learned. Use AI to generate a 3-question share template + rotating schedule
- **1:1 Coaching Questions** — embed these into standing 1:1s:
  - "Where could AI remove friction in your work this week?"
  - "What's one task you'll try it on before our next 1:1?"
  - "What did you learn from using AI this week — even if it didn't work?"
- **Scheduled Slack nudge** — set a recurring weekly nudge to your team channel: "Drop one AI win, attempt, or lesson this week — big or small."

### Monthly
- **AI Opportunities Session** — create or revisit a team process map using an AI workflow mapper; ask "where's the friction?" monthly review builds the habit over time
- **AI Show, Don't Tell** — one person goes deeper on what they've been building or trying. 20–30 min. Not a polished presentation — just show the actual tool, workflow, or output
- **Monthly block for AI play** — block 2–4 hours. No agenda. Explore, experiment, ask for help. Make the space explicit on the calendar

### Quarterly
- **Strategize 1–2 function-specific use cases** — pick the highest-impact thing your function could do with AI; scope it, pilot it, measure what happened. This is the highest-leverage manager activity
- **Mini Hackathon** — half or full day; bring a real problem, use AI to prototype something, share what was built
- **Longer learning sessions** — reach out to learn@thumbtack.com for facilitated sessions, workshops, or function-specific programming

## Coaching by Learning Mode

Use this table to guide how you coach individual team members based on where they are in the Explorer → Operator → Builder journey. These are not performance tiers — they describe engagement mode right now.

| Mode | Manager Goal | Coach Moves | Route To |
|------|-------------|-------------|----------|
| **Explorer** | Reduce cold-start friction; build confidence through small wins | Surface fears or concerns; do one AI thing together in a 1:1 (pick a real task, try it live); give one safe starting task + prompt starter; pair with an Operator buddy; celebrate the attempt, not just the outcome | TackStart — 30 Days of Exploring AI |
| **Operator** | Improve discernment; add use cases; move toward workflow integration | Encourage standardizing one pattern into a repeatable system; encourage sharing learnings in #ai-lab; look for handoff opportunities (what could be automated?); pair with a Builder buddy | TackShift — 30 Days of Operating Better with AI |
| **Builder** | Focus energy on the right bets; stay safe; prevent burnout | Stay closely looped in; recognize and surface their work to leadership; encourage cross-team sharing; connect the dots org-wide; protect their time from being overloaded with ad hoc requests | AI Champions Program; advanced L&D; encourage outside TT learning |

## Addressing Common Team Concerns

### "AI is going to take my job."
**Acknowledge it:** This fear is valid and understandable — don't minimize it.

**What to say:** "Roles evolve — that's always been true at Thumbtack. The goal isn't fewer people doing less. It's each of us doing more of the work that matters. Each of us helps shape what the next version of our craft looks like."

**What to do next:** Co-design one experiment together that increases leverage without removing ownership. Make it theirs.

---

### "I don't have time for this."
**Acknowledge it:** This is often true — they're busy and learning new tools feels like another thing to do.

**What to say:** "We're not adding work — we're building skills that save time. I want us to carve out X minutes a week for 4 weeks and see what changes. Pick one workflow, one safe task, and let's check back in."

**What to do next:** Pick one specific task together; schedule a brief review in the next 1:1.

---

### "I don't trust the output."
**Acknowledge it:** Healthy skepticism is a feature, not a bug.

**What to say:** "That's actually the right instinct. Output quality depends heavily on how you use the tool and what you're asking it for. It's not magic — it's an ongoing work in progress, and your judgment matters. Let's talk about what you tried and what didn't feel right."

**What to do next:** Route to TackStart or TackShift; work through a specific example together where skepticism was warranted and where the output was actually useful.

---

### "I'm worried I'll use it wrong."
**Acknowledge it:** Normal. Everyone starts here.

**What to say:** "Progress over perfection — always. The goal is learning, and that means some experiments won't work. I'd rather you try and tell me what happened than wait until you feel confident."

**What to do next:** Model experimentation and failure yourself; share something that didn't work before sharing something that did.

---

### "Is this safe? Is it allowed?"
**What to say:** "Let me check and make sure we're aligned." Don't guess.

**What to do next:** Ensure the person has completed AI in the Workplace Training (required for all employees; available in AI Hub). Check with IT or Legal if there's a specific data or governance concern. Route anything involving proprietary data or customer PII through the AI/ML legal review process.

---

### "I'm not technical — this isn't for me."
**Acknowledge it:** The tools are designed for non-technical users. You don't need to code.

**What to say:** "You don't need to be technical at all. The tools are designed for people who think in words, not code. Let me show you what non-technical people are already doing with this."

**What to do next:** Give one non-technical starting task (summarize a document, draft a memo, analyze a spreadsheet); pair with a non-technical peer who's already using AI; reach out to learn@thumbtack.com for support.

---

### "AI is biased and unethical — I have real concerns."
**Acknowledge it:** These are legitimate concerns worth taking seriously.

**What to say:** "I hear you, and these are real issues. The goal isn't to pretend the risks don't exist — it's to use these tools intentionally, critically, and responsibly. That kind of thoughtful use actually matters, and your instinct to question things is exactly what keeps us using AI well."

**What to do next:** Route to AI in the Workplace Training and responsible use guidelines. Have a real conversation about the specific concern — don't shut it down.

## 5 Team Experimentation Formats

### Format Selector
| If the goal is... | Use this format |
|------------------|----------------|
| Solve a real workflow challenge directly | AI Workflow Mapping Session (60 min) |
| Generate ideas quickly + create momentum and energy | AI Brainstorm / Mini Hackathon (~2 hrs) |
| Build comfort and reps for newer users | Show, Don't Tell (30–60 min) |
| Support an individual on a specific blocker | Office Hours / Buddy Pairings |
| Dedicated upskilling anchor (offsite or AI Day) | AI Day (half or full day) — see offsite-templates.md |

---

### Format 1: AI Workflow Mapping Session (60 min)
**Goal:** Dissect a real workflow together, spot AI opportunities, and leave with one scoped experiment.

**Prep (before the session):**
- Share a workflow map template with participants 10–15 min before or at the start
- Ask everyone to come with one workflow they do regularly

**Agenda:**
1. **Icebreaker + reflection** (5 min): "What's one thing you've tried with AI in the last two weeks — even if it didn't work?"
2. **Complete the workflow mapping matrix** (20 min): As a team, map out the workflow step-by-step. For each step: What does it involve? How long does it take? Where does friction live? Could AI help here?
3. **Discuss** (10 min): What opportunities exist? What's the low-hanging fruit? What would make the biggest difference if it worked?
4. **Choose 1 experiment** (5 min): Vote or decide: what's the one thing we're going to try?
5. **Go deeper on that experiment** (10 min): Map the current state in detail. What AI tool would apply? DVFR check — is this Desirable (would the team use it?), Viable (worth the time?), Feasible (actually buildable?), Responsible (safe and appropriate?)?
6. **Land on a plan** (5 min): Owner. Scope. Tool. Definition of success. Share-back date. Put it in writing.
7. **Next steps + accountability** (5 min): Who's doing what by when? When do we debrief?

---

### Format 2: AI Brainstorm / Mini Hackathon (~2 hrs)
**Goal:** Solve a specific, real problem using AI — and build something (or at least a prototype) together.

**Prep (before the session):**
- Choose the problem in advance (or bring 2 options and vote live at the start)
- Gather 2–3 real artifacts that relate to the problem: a doc, a data file, a process description
- Make sure everyone has access to at least one AI tool

**Agenda:**
1. **Icebreaker** (5 min): "What's the most surprising thing AI has helped you do recently?"
2. **Understand the need** (15 min): Walk through the problem. Draft a "How Might We" statement together: "How might we [solve X] so that [outcome]?"
3. **Gather inspiration + generate ideas** (20 min): Use AI to help brainstorm solutions. Try at least two different approaches. Write down the best ideas.
4. **Pick one idea** (10 min): DVFR check — Desirable, Viable, Feasible, Responsible? Vote or decide.
5. **Prototype** (40 min): Build it. Use Claude Code, ChatGPT, Gemini — whatever is available. It doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to exist.
6. **Reflection + share learnings** (15 min): Show what you built. What worked? What surprised you? What would you do differently?
7. **Next steps** (5 min): Is this worth continuing? Who owns it? What's the next step?

---

### Format 3: Show, Don't Tell (30–60 min)
**Goal:** Build comfort and momentum through live demos and guided practice — especially for teams where most people are in Explorer mode.

**Prep (before the session):**
- Pick 1–3 tools to feature (keep it simple)
- Make sure everyone has tool access set up before the session
- Choose one challenge or task everyone will try during the session

**Agenda:**
1. **Icebreaker** (5 min): "On a scale of 1–5, how comfortable do you feel with AI tools right now? Be honest."
2. **Context + tool framing** (5–10 min): Why are we doing this? What's available? Set the tone: "Progress over perfection. We're here to try things, not to be experts."
3. **Demo the tools** (10–15 min): Show the tool in action. Don't just describe it — do something real with it, on the screen, out loud. Show the process, not just the result. Include something that doesn't work perfectly.
4. **Challenge time** (10–20 min): Give everyone a task to try on their own (or in pairs). Use something from their actual daily work — not a made-up example.
5. **Share process + results** (10 min): What did you try? What happened? What surprised you?
6. **Capture learnings** (5 min): What are the 2–3 takeaways we want to remember?
7. **Close + next steps**: What's each person going to try before the next team meeting? How will we share back?

---

### Format 4: Office Hours / Buddy Pairings
**Goal:** Support a team member on a specific blocker or task in a smaller, lower-pressure setting.

**Best for:** Individuals who are stuck; people who won't speak up in a group session; situations where the blocker is very task-specific.

**Structure:**
1. **Identify the blocker** before the session — what is the specific thing they're trying to do and where are they stuck?
2. **Pair on the task live** — try it together in real time. One person drives; one person asks questions. Don't just explain it; do it with them.
3. **Capture what worked** — write down the prompt, the approach, or the workflow that helped. Make it repeatable.
4. **Define next steps** — what will they try on their own before you check back in?

**Buddy pairing tip:** Match Explorers with Operators, and Operators with Builders. The goal isn't formal mentorship — it's enough of a gap that there's something to learn, not so much of a gap that it's intimidating.

---

### Format 5: AI Day (Half Day or Full Day)
**Goal:** A dedicated, structured upskilling event that anchors momentum and gives the team shared vocabulary and experience.

**Best for:** Teams with upcoming offsite time; teams that need a reset or a real starting point; teams with a mix of stages who need to build a shared foundation.

**Structure:**
- **Framing session** — why AI, why now, what Thumbtack expects; set agreements (progress over perfection, learn out loud)
- **Skill-building block** — guided practice with 2–4 tools; start from real work, not demos
- **Application block** — each person (or small group) tries something for their actual function or workflow
- **Share-out** — what did you build? What surprised you? What's one thing you're taking back?
- **Commitments close** — each person names one thing they'll continue after today
