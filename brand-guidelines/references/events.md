# Event Brand Guidelines

Thumbtack focuses on the needs of event guests. Guests do not need branded stress balls.

## Event Collateral
When designing event collateral, lean into Thumbtack's refreshing and realistic attributes.
- **Use**: Earthy, textured materials like wood, ink, and matte paper
- **Avoid**: Artificial, tacky materials like foamcore or glossy vinyl

## Venues
- Choose open, airy gallery spaces — a great canvas for bringing the brand to life
- Do not rent spaces that are too dark or use colored lighting — it should not feel like a bar or club
- Choose a venue that is easy to find and navigate
- Lighter woods like birch add brightness and warmth to event spaces

## Decor
- Keep decor simple and functional
- Only brand key focal points or gathering places
- No cheap decor like balloons or crepe paper — this is not a birthday party
- Consider using pro portraits to highlight pros' accomplishments and unique stories

## Wayfinding
- Create a simple, branded navigation system that guests can spot quickly
- Feature brand colors and icons
- Include essentials: restrooms, exits, key areas
- Use icons the same way they are used in product — for clear wayfinding and subtle branding

## Swag
- Quality over quantity — pick meaningful items that add real value
- Choose things people will actually use
- Avoid generic corporate swag (stress balls, cheap pens)

## Event Don'ts
- Do not give guests unnecessary clutter to carry around
- Not everything has to be branded — resist the urge; guests do not need to eat the logo
- Watch out for cheap design details like ugly carpet and loud wallpaper
- Do not go overboard with signage or branding — this is not Times Square
