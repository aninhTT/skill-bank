# Photography Guidelines

## Lifestyle Photography

### The Thumbtack Home
Lifestyle photography reflects real people and real homes. It captures the experience of caring for a home in a way that feels authentic and approachable. The Thumbtack home is warm, relatable, and distinctly personal.

Real homes means seeing subtle signs of daily life: the mug on the counter, the toys tucked into a corner, the family photos on the fridge. Homeowners should see themselves and their homes reflected in the photography.

### Composition and Subjects
Lifestyle photography is layered storytelling — an opportunity to showcase the details that make each home unique. Use lighting and composition to reveal those details. Do not clean up unique flaws or make anything feel staged. Enhance what exists and bring unique qualities to the forefront.

### Lighting
Show subjects near natural light to add tonal contrast and warmth. Aim for:
- Soft shadows
- Full dynamic range
- Natural light as the primary light source

### Pro Wardrobe and Props
Pros are passionate and know their trades. Pro lifestyle photography should reflect this: real professionals at work in real spaces.
- Depict correct apparel, equipment, and technique to enhance credibility
- Avoid costume-y apparel and inaccurate props — these are distracting and harm brand integrity
- Images should feel natural and organic
- Even when using stock photography, avoid staged compositions

### Textures of Home
Clean white space is core to the visual brand. Photography of bright, white home textures (marble, tile, linen) can be used as background patterns behind text. This preserves white space while subtly nodding to the home.

### Lifestyle Photography Don'ts
- Do not use images that feel overly staged — anything that looks like a furniture catalog is off-brand
- Do not use images that feel overly messy — the home should feel lived in, not neglected
- Do not let home textures overpower the text — they should always be secondary
- Do not put textures over a color — they are meant to recede and function as white space

## Category Photography

### Purpose
Category photos represent thousands of services on Thumbtack. They should make the category obvious at first glance.

### Composition and Subjects
- Focus on projects over people
- Keep compositions simple — focal point square in the frame, cropped to fill the image
- Use negative space and depth of field to direct the eye

### Lighting and Location
Category photos are snapshots of a real project, captured in action.
- Focus on signs of life or the messy, in-between stages of a project
- Shoot with plenty of natural light for warmth, tonal contrast, and depth

### Wardrobe and Props
- Use authentic props and clothing that clearly represent the category
- Pick accurate tools for the featured project
- Avoid dressing subjects in cartoonish or stock costumes

### Category Photography Don'ts
- Do not make compositions feel overly staged or locations too polished
- Do not shoot settings so wide that they overwhelm the subject, project, or category
- Do not overuse shallow depth of field — it is too dreamy for the realistic brand
- Do not use images that lose their edge when placed on white
- Do not over-edit photos — save the filters for selfies
- When featuring people, capture natural expressions and body language
