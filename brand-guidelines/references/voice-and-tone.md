# Voice and Tone

## Voice Attributes

Thumbtack's voice creates a feeling of familiarity and good judgment. The reader should sense a real person on the other end of the words. Three attributes define how Thumbtack sounds:

### Relatable
In other words: approachable, generous, empathetic.
And definitely not: neutral, impersonal, perfect.

Thumbtack communicates like a trusted neighbor — someone who understands the stress of home projects and genuinely wants to help. Copy should feel warm and human, never robotic or corporate.

### Grounded
In other words: reasonable, realistic, pragmatic.
And definitely not: lofty, highly aspirational, slick.

Thumbtack avoids grand promises and over-the-top language. Keep it real. If something is hard, acknowledge it. Speak plainly about what Thumbtack does and does not do.

### Natural
In other words: unaffected, conversational, subtly playful.
And definitely not: cutesy, punny or hammy, overly crafted.

Writing should feel easy and unforced. A little personality is great — a pun avalanche is not. Read copy out loud; if it sounds like something a real person would say, it is on-brand.

## Tone Variations

If voice is the brand's personality, tone is all the different ways that personality is expressed. Choose the tone that fits the moment:

| Tone | When to use |
|------|------------|
| **Celebratory** | Wins, milestones, achievements — for both customers and pros |
| **Instructive** | How-to content, onboarding, product education |
| **Playful** | Social media, casual marketing, moments of surprise and delight |
| **Reassuring** | Trust-building, security messaging, handling concerns |
| **Encouraging** | Motivating pros to grow, helping customers take the first step |
| **Supportive** | Customer service, help center, troubleshooting |
| **Helpful** | Practical guidance, recommendations, tips and advice |

## Audience-Specific Positioning

### For Homeowners (Customers)
Thumbtack gives homeowners personalized guidance on what to do and when to do it, as well as who to hire from a growing community of 300,000 local service businesses. Copy should emphasize:
- Convenience — easy to find the right pro
- Confidence — trustworthy matches, verified reviews
- Care — helping them protect and improve their home

### For Home Professionals (Pros)
At Thumbtack, local professionals find the profitable jobs they need to grow their businesses. Copy should emphasize:
- Growth — more jobs, more revenue
- Control — target the right jobs, set preferences
- Achievement — accomplish more, one job at a time

### Elevator Pitch (Full)
Thumbtack is a technology company helping millions of people confidently care for and improve their homes. Homeowners get personalized guidance on what to do and when to do it as well as who to hire from a community of 300,000 local service businesses. Every day in every county of the U.S., people turn to Thumbtack to complete small fixes, routine maintenance, and bigger improvements. With over 12 million 5-star projects and counting, Thumbtack helps homeowners and home professionals accomplish more.

## Writing Principles

- Write like a real person — read it aloud to test
- Be direct — say what you mean without filler
- Respect the reader's time — shorter is usually better
- Avoid jargon — if a term needs explaining, rethink it
- Match the moment — celebratory copy at a funeral is bad judgment; reassuring copy at a launch party is a missed opportunity
- Stay grounded — Thumbtack solves real problems; do not oversell

## Quick Copy Examples

### On-Brand
- "Over 300,000 local pros. One easy way to find the right one." — Grounded, specific, simple.
- "Your home's to-do list isn't going to finish itself. But the right pro can help." — Relatable, natural, subtly playful.
- "Pros on Thumbtack completed 12 million 5-star projects. Yours could be next." — Data-backed, encouraging, not lofty.

### Off-Brand
- "The #1 REVOLUTIONARY platform transforming home services!!!" — Lofty, hype-driven, slick.
- "Unlock your home's full potential with our cutting-edge technology!" — Aspirational buzzwords, not grounded.
- "Don't be a fool — hire a pro on Thumbtack before it's too late!" — Pressuring, not relatable or natural.
