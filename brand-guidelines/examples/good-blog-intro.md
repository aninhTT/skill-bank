# Example: Blog Post Intro

## On-Brand (Good)

> **3 takeaways from the 2025 Pro Summit**
>
> Last month, hundreds of home service professionals gathered for Thumbtack's annual Pro Summit. They shared what's working, what's changing, and where the industry is headed. Here are three things that stood out.

**Why it works**:
- **Grounded**: Opens with a real event and real participants — no hype
- **Natural**: Conversational tone, reads like a person recapping what happened
- **Relatable**: "They shared what's working" — centers the pros, not the company
- Numbered structure promised in headline — clear and scannable
- No exclamation marks, no superlatives
- Data/specifics come in the body, not crammed into the intro

---

## Off-Brand (Bad)

> **The HOTTEST Trends in Home Services You NEED to Know About!!!**
>
> The home services industry is being DISRUPTED like never before! At our INCREDIBLE Pro Summit, we unveiled game-changing insights that will transform the way you think about your business. These cutting-edge takeaways are absolutely essential for every pro who wants to stay ahead of the curve! 🔥🔥🔥

**Why it fails**:
- "HOTTEST", "INCREDIBLE", "DISRUPTED" — lofty ALL CAPS hype, not grounded
- "game-changing", "cutting-edge", "transform" — slick buzzwords Thumbtack avoids
- "we unveiled" — self-congratulatory, centers the company over the community
- "stay ahead of the curve" — cliche, not natural
- Triple exclamation marks and fire emoji — excessive, off-brand
- No real information or data — just hype

---

## Key Rules for Blog Posts

- **Structure**: Title → intro (2–3 sentences) → numbered/headed sections → soft CTA
- **Voice**: Conversational yet authoritative — a knowledgeable friend, not a salesperson
- **Data**: Lead with real stats, specifics, or observations — not vague claims
- **Tone**: Usually Instructive, Helpful, or Encouraging
- **CTAs**: End with a natural next step, not a hard sell
- **Length**: Intro should be 2–4 sentences max — get to the substance quickly
