# Example: Social Post

## On-Brand (Good)

> Over 12 million 5-star projects and counting. Behind every one is a homeowner who found the right pro — and a pro who found the right job. That's Thumbtack at work.

**Why it works**:
- **Grounded**: Leads with a real stat, not a promise
- **Relatable**: Centers both homeowners and pros
- **Natural**: Reads like something a real person would say
- Soft CTA (no "Sign up NOW!")
- No hashtag overload

---

## Off-Brand (Bad)

> 🎉🎉🎉 We're REVOLUTIONIZING the home services industry!!! 🏠💪 Thumbtack is THE game-changing platform that's disrupting how you find pros! Don't miss out — sign up TODAY! #HomeServices #GameChanger #Disruption #Innovation #Thumbtack #HomePros

**Why it fails**:
- "Revolutionizing", "game-changing", "disrupting" — lofty and slick, not grounded
- Excessive exclamation marks and emoji — not natural
- Self-congratulatory ("THE platform") — not relatable
- Hard sell CTA ("Don't miss out") — pressuring, not helpful
- 6 hashtags — cluttered (limit to 1–3)
- ALL CAPS hype words — off-brand tone

---

## Key Rules for Social Posts

- **Platform limits**: X/Twitter ~280 chars, LinkedIn ~3000 chars, Instagram caption ~2200 chars
- **Hashtags**: 1–3 max, relevant and specific
- **CTAs**: Soft and natural ("Learn more at..." not "SIGN UP NOW!!!")
- **Emoji**: Restrained — 0–2 per post, never leading with emoji strings
- **Tone**: Usually Celebratory, Encouraging, or Helpful
- **Image pairing**: Use lifestyle or category photography, never stock-looking images
